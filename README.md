# Populism-and-the-2024-French-Election
# The Rise of Populist Voting: The French 2024 Election

## Overview

This project examines the rise of populist radical right voting in France during the 2024 election through the case study of the Rassemblement National (RN).

The analysis combines:
- economic voting theory;
- issue voting;
- political distrust;
- identity-based explanations of populism;
- quantitative ESS data analysis.

The paper argues that RN's electoral success is best understood as the interaction between economic insecurity, cultural conflict, and declining political trust rather than purely material explanations alone.

## Research Areas

- Populism
- Political behaviour
- Political economy
- Immigration attitudes
- Political trust
- European politics
- French politics

## Methods

- Literature review
- Political theory
- Quantitative ESS analysis
- Logistic regression
- Comparative political analysis

## Key Findings

- Immigration threat perceptions were the strongest predictor of right-wing alignment.
- Economic dissatisfaction alone did not sufficiently explain populist radical right voting.
- Political distrust played a major role in structuring anti-elite sentiment.
- RN’s success reflected broader transformations in democratic representation and political identity formation.
